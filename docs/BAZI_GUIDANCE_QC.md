# Bazi guidance fact-check standard

This standard governs the internal Day Master knowledge library and every free-summary report generated from it. Private references may inform the library, but their names, wording, and provenance must never appear in a customer report.

## Verified foundations

- The Day Master is the Heavenly Stem of the Day Pillar.
- There are ten Day Masters: the Yin and Yang expression of each of the Five Elements.
- Yin or Yang is polarity; it does not by itself mean weak or strong.
- Day Master strength describes the chart's energetic support, not character, confidence, health, worth, or resilience.
- The Month Branch and season are essential to a strength assessment. A season-only result is a simplified screening classification and must not be presented as a complete chart-strength judgment.
- A Day Master summary describes one lens within the chart. It must not imply that it represents the full Bazi chart.

## Required internal depth

Maintain a separate knowledge profile for each of the ten Day Masters. Each profile should contain:

1. Classical image and polarity distinction.
2. Core motivations and preferred way of engaging.
3. Constructive expressions that a parent may observe.
4. Possible pressure or overextension expressions.
5. Possible under-supported expressions.
6. Age-appropriate examples at home, in learning, and with peers.
7. Parenting responses that may help, with alternatives for different situations.
8. Clear limits: what cannot be concluded from the Day Master alone.
9. A fact status for every point: verified principle, bounded interpretation, or practical parenting suggestion.

Strong and weak variants must add context to the same Day Master; they must not become fixed personality labels. They should give the language model enough verified material to select from without encouraging it to invent traits.

## Customer-writing rules

- Use cautious, observational language: “may,” “often,” “parents may notice,” and “can be more likely when.”
- Give recognisable examples, but never claim that a behaviour must occur.
- Explain the Day Master once, then relate later sections back naturally without repeating technical labels in every card.
- Treat sensitivities as support needs, not defects.
- Keep technical derivation internal unless a short explanation materially helps the parent.
- Present parenting actions as options to try, not outcomes guaranteed by Bazi.
- Never use the chart to diagnose, predict certainty, assign moral value, or override direct knowledge of the child.

## Expert QC gates

A report must be withheld or replaced by the safe deterministic version when any of the following is true:

- A claim is not traceable to an allowed Day Master knowledge point.
- Yin/Yang polarity is confused with strength.
- A season-only strength classification is described as a complete strength analysis.
- Generic Five Element wording is used as if it distinguishes the ten Day Masters.
- Parenting advice is presented as a chart fact or guaranteed solution.
- Traits from structures, profile stars, Ten Gods, or pillar interactions are introduced into the Day Master-only summary.
- The prose is fatalistic, negative, diagnostic, overly technical, repetitive, impersonal, or source-revealing.

## Current audit result

The existing five-element fallback is directionally useful but too broad for final expert status. It does not adequately distinguish the Yin and Yang Day Masters, and some behavioural statements extend beyond the directly verified elemental imagery. It should remain a temporary safe fallback while the detailed ten-Day-Master library is built and reviewed under this standard.
